# Investor Narrative

## Investment Thesis

Global AI infrastructure growth is driving structural increases in:
- Electricity demand
- GPU infrastructure demand
- Transmission infrastructure requirements
- Cooling efficiency requirements
- Fiber resiliency needs

Existing hyperscale regions increasingly face:
- Grid saturation
- Transmission congestion
- Rising operational costs
- Land scarcity
- Cooling constraints

These pressures create opportunities for new infrastructure regions capable of supporting long-term AI and hyperscale growth.

## Strategic Infrastructure Opportunity

The Alaska AI infrastructure opportunity combines:
- Dispatchable baseload power
- Potential dedicated transmission
- Cold-climate cooling efficiency
- Large-scale land availability
- Water resource availability
- Fiber diversification potential
- Strategic Arctic positioning

This combination creates a differentiated infrastructure profile compared to traditional hyperscale markets.

## AI Infrastructure Market Drivers

Key long-term growth drivers include:
- Generative AI expansion
- GPU compute demand
- AI training workloads
- Sovereign AI initiatives
- National AI competitiveness
- Defense and secure compute requirements

These drivers support sustained long-term infrastructure demand.

## Fiber and Resiliency Opportunity

Existing interstate subsea systems serving Alaska face:
- Long-term capacity exhaustion risks
- Aging infrastructure
- Cascadia seismic exposure
- Limited route diversity

New Arctic fiber diversification initiatives may support:
- Improved resiliency
- Geo-diverse routing
- Strategic AI infrastructure growth
- National security alignment

## Strategic Customer Potential

Potential customer categories include:
- Hyperscalers
- GPU cloud providers
- Sovereign AI initiatives
- Semiconductor ecosystem companies
- Defense-related compute workloads
- Telecom and infrastructure operators

## Competitive Differentiation

Compared to traditional hyperscale regions, Alaska may offer:
- Lower cooling costs
- Dedicated power potential
- Expansion scalability
- Lower infrastructure competition
- Strategic Arctic positioning
- Potential long-term power stability

## Long-Term Infrastructure Positioning

The project may be positioned as:
- Long-duration strategic infrastructure
- AI infrastructure platform
- Energy + compute convergence asset
- Arctic digital infrastructure corridor
- Resilient compute infrastructure platform

## Strategic Conclusion

The convergence of:
- AI infrastructure growth
- Power scarcity
- Transmission bottlenecks
- Cooling efficiency pressures
- Fiber resiliency concerns
- National security priorities

creates a long-term strategic infrastructure opportunity for next-generation AI infrastructure development in Alaska.
