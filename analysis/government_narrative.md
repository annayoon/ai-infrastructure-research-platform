# Government Narrative

## Strategic Context

Artificial intelligence infrastructure is increasingly being recognized as critical national infrastructure tied to:
- Economic competitiveness
- National security
- Energy security
- Digital sovereignty
- Resilient communications infrastructure

Governments are increasingly concerned with the ability of existing power, transmission, and digital infrastructure systems to support long-term AI growth.

## U.S. Government Alignment

### DOE Alignment

The U.S. Department of Energy increasingly recognizes:
- AI-driven electricity demand growth
- Transmission bottlenecks
- Grid modernization requirements
- Strategic importance of resilient infrastructure
- Need for onsite and dispatchable generation

TECC’s infrastructure model aligns with these priorities through:
- Dispatchable baseload power
- Potential dedicated transmission
- AI infrastructure support
- Long-term infrastructure scalability

### DoD / National Security Alignment

Alaska is strategically important for:
- Arctic security
- Pacific defense operations
- Space and satellite infrastructure
- Secure communications
- Resilient compute infrastructure

Fiber diversification and resilient compute infrastructure align with:
- National defense resiliency objectives
- Critical infrastructure redundancy
- Arctic strategic positioning
- Secure AI and compute growth

- Economic diversification

## Korean Government Alignment

Korea faces increasing pressure related to:
- AI compute demand growth

The project may support:

## Japan and APAC Strategic Relevance

Japan and broader APAC markets are experiencing:
- AI infrastructure growth
- GPU compute demand
- Digital infrastructure decentralization
- Energy and cooling pressures

Alaska’s geographic positioning may support:
- APAC-U.S. infrastructure diversification
- Arctic fiber diversification
- Long-term resilient compute infrastructure

## Strategic Conclusion

The convergence of:
- AI infrastructure growth
- Power and transmission constraints
- Fiber resiliency concerns
- Arctic strategic relevance
- National security priorities
- AI competitiveness pressures

creates a strategic opportunity for government participation and support in next-generation AI infrastructure development.
