# Market Sounding Strategy

## Objective

The primary objective of market sounding is to:
- Validate commercial demand for Alaska-based AI infrastructure
- Identify qualified APAC and U.S. customer prospects
- Assess compute, power, cooling, and connectivity requirements
- Identify potential anchor tenants and strategic partners
- Evaluate willingness to commit to long-term compute or infrastructure agreements

## Strategic Market Focus

The market sounding effort should prioritize:
- AI infrastructure operators
- GPU cloud providers
- Hyperscalers
- Sovereign AI initiatives
- Semiconductor ecosystem companies
- Defense and secure compute workloads
- Telecom and subsea infrastructure stakeholders

## Market Sounding Themes

### Power
- Required MW scale
- Long-term power pricing expectations
- Dedicated generation interest
- Off-grid / resilient compute interest

### Connectivity
- Latency sensitivity
- Redundancy requirements
- Geo-diverse routing interest
- International connectivity requirements

### Cooling and Sustainability
- Cooling efficiency priorities
- PUE targets
- Water usage concerns
- ESG and sustainability requirements

### Operational Requirements
- Security and compliance
- Reliability expectations
- Uptime requirements
- Expansion scalability

## Customer Qualification Criteria

Potential customers should be evaluated based on:
- Compute intensity
- Power demand
- Long-term infrastructure needs
- Strategic fit with Alaska advantages
- Ability to commit to long-term agreements
- Expansion roadmap

## Expected Outputs

The market sounding effort should produce:
- Customer target list
- Stakeholder engagement map
- Interview summaries
- Demand validation evidence
- Potential LOI / MOU opportunities
- Compute offtake indicators
- Strategic partnership opportunities
