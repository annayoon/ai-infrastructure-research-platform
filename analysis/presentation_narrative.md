# Executive Presentation Narrative

# Core Strategic Narrative

AI infrastructure growth is rapidly outpacing the capabilities of existing hyperscale infrastructure regions.

Power availability, transmission scalability, cooling efficiency, and fiber resiliency are emerging as critical bottlenecks for long-term AI infrastructure expansion.

At the same time, governments increasingly recognize AI infrastructure as strategic national infrastructure tied to:
- Economic competitiveness
- National security
- Sovereign AI capability
- Critical digital infrastructure resilience

---

# Structural Infrastructure Shift

Traditional hyperscale regions increasingly face:
- Grid congestion
- Large-load interconnection delays
- Cooling and water constraints
- Infrastructure saturation
- Fiber resiliency concerns

These structural pressures are creating demand for new AI infrastructure regions capable of supporting:
- Long-term scalable power
- Resilient compute
- Efficient cooling
- Geo-diverse connectivity

---

# Why Alaska

Alaska offers a differentiated infrastructure profile due to:
- Potential dispatchable baseload generation
- Dedicated transmission opportunities
- Cold-climate cooling efficiency
- Large-scale developable land
- Abundant water resources
- Lower infrastructure competition
- Strategic Arctic geography

---

# Fiber and Resiliency Opportunity

Existing Alaska-serving subsea systems face:
- Aging infrastructure
- Long-term capacity exhaustion risks
- Cascadia seismic exposure
- Limited route diversity

Arctic fiber diversification initiatives may support:
- Geo-diverse routing
- Improved resiliency
- Long-term AI infrastructure scalability
- APAC-U.S. infrastructure diversification

---
