TRANSCO
  • A transmission-owning entity
  • Regulated entity that owns, constructs, and maintains
    transmission lines used to transmit wholesale power
  • May handle power dispatch and coordination
  • Secures financing
  • Executes projects




                                                            12
ISO
• Description
   • Owns no assets
   • Neutral party responsible for planning, management and control of the
     electric transmission grid in a region

• Characteristics
   • Ensures non-discriminatory access
   • Possesses operational authority for all transmission facilities under the ISO’s
     control
   • Exclusive authority to maintain short-term reliability
   • Operates under a regulatory compact
   • Plans, conditions and approves projects

COULD BE RAILBELT MODEL
•   TRANSCO = Alaska Railbelt Cooperative Transmission & Electric Company
    (ARCTEC)
     • Non-discriminatory open access
     • Existing assets from transmission providers are pooled for joint use with
       full cost recovery for all users
     • Universal rates based on system load that will encourage economic
       transfer of generation across network
•   Transition to full implementation
     • Single control area
     • Economic dispatch
     • Centralized planning for all future transmission

CONCLUSION
•   Without a program to rectify transmission shortcomings asap
     • Cost of electricity will continue to rise
     • Possible savings will be forfeited
     • Alaska will not achieve its alternative energy goal
     • Railbelt will be captive to Cook Inlet natural gas pricing and supply issues
•   NEXT STEPS
     • Continue 50/50 deal between State and utilities for Bradley Lake upgrades
     • Reliability standards and Open Access proceeding through RCA process
     • ARCTEC proceeding with TRANSCO/ISO options
